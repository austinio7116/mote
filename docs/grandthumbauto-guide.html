01What is it?

Grand Thumb Auto drops you into a living city with no rules and a fistful of nothing. Steal any car, run jobs for shady contacts off the payphones, and see how much cash you can bank before the law — or the army — puts you down. There's no game over that matters: get wasted or busted and you're back on the street, minus your weapons, chasing a bigger number.

Press A to hit the streets
On foot — the cursor prompts your next move
Every car in the city is yours for the taking
A new city every game. The whole map — rivers, bridges, the road network, districts, parks, the docks — is generated fresh each time you start a new game. No two runs share a street plan, so the guide below is about how the city works, not a memorised map.

02Controls

Eight buttons, and they mean different things on foot and behind the wheel. The prompt at the bottom of the screen tells you what A and B do right now.

On foot

D-padWalk. You face the way you move — that's the way you'll shoot. AEnter the nearest car (steal it), or use a payphone / gun shop you're standing on. BAttack with your current weapon — a fist if you're holding nothing. RBSwitch to your next owned weapon. MENUOpen the full city map (pauses play; D-pad pans, B pans faster).

In a car

RBAccelerate. LBBrake — soft on a tap, harder the longer you hold. Once fully stopped, LB becomes reverse. LEFT / RIGHTSteer. No grip below walking pace — you need to be rolling to turn. BFire your weapon from the window (drive-by), or the main gun if you're in a tank. AStep out of the car.
Behind the wheel is real physics. Cars have weight, grip and momentum — they slide, spin out and glance off each other. Heavy muscle cars need room to turn; a jacked car keeps the speed it had when you took it.

03Your first few minutes

You start on a pavement with empty pockets. The rhythm of the game is simple: get wheels, get a job, get paid, buy guns, cause trouble, escape, repeat.

  1. Grab a car. There's a parked one right next to you at the start — walk up and press A. Any moving car works too: press A beside it and the driver bails out.
  2. Find a payphone. Open the map (MENU) — markers you've discovered show up on it. Drive to a phone, get out, stand on it and press A to take a job.
  3. Do the job, watch the timer. Most jobs are against the clock. The pay (and a running chain bonus for back-to-back successes) shows in the HUD.
  4. Spend it. Roll into a gun shop to arm up, or a pay-n-spray to shake the cops. Drive stolen cars to the docks to sell them to the fence.
  5. Don't die broke. Getting wasted or busted keeps your cash and best score but strips your weapons — bank progress by staying alive and buying what lasts.

04The city

The map is a real town: a ring road and arterials, dense blocks of buildings, one or more rivers crossed by bridges, waterfront, parks, and a scatter of useful places. It's all one connected road network — you can drive anywhere.

05Cars & money

Cars are transport, weapons and income all at once. There's a whole fleet on the streets — hatchbacks, muscle cars, sports cars, taxis, ambulances, buses, even a fire truck — each with its own weight, speed and handling.

Making cash

Three taps flow money in: jobs from the payphones (the biggest earner, with a chain multiplier for streaks), selling stolen cars at the docks (rarer and fancier motors fetch more — a sports car beats a hatchback, a tank is the jackpot), and cash pickups dropped by the people and caches you shake down.

Losing the cops

The pay-n-spray is your panic button: drive in with any wanted level and $100 wipes it clean and resprays the car so the heat can't recognise you. It also repairs the bodywork. Cheaper than dying — dying costs you every gun you were carrying.

Sell what you don't need. Finished a driving job? Don't abandon the car — limp it to the docks and cash it in. A pristine motor sells for full price; a wreck sells for nothing.

07The wanted system

Commit a crime where the law can see or hear it and stars light up top-right. The more stars, the harder the response — and it keeps climbing while you keep offending. Crimes nobody witnesses go unpunished, so a quiet back-street mugging costs you nothing.

★★★★★CLEAN

No heat. A lone patrol car cruises and a beat cop walks — they're the witnesses. Slip past them and you stay invisible.

★★★★★1 STAR

Squad cars start hunting you. Outrun them or duck into a pay-n-spray.

★★★★★3 STARS

They arrive in pairs and press harder. Officers bail out on foot to shoot — and to arrest you if you're on foot and they reach you.

★★★★★4 STARS

Roadblocks are thrown across the street ahead when you're speeding. Pick another route or crash through.

★★★★★5 STARS

The army rolls in. A hostile tank joins the hunt and lobs shells at you from range. Now you're really in trouble.

Two ways down. Wasted (health gone) or Busted (a cop grabs you on foot) both send you back to the street. You keep your cash and best score, but lose the weapons you were carrying — so cash out and re-arm before you push your luck.

Build a chain. Finish jobs back-to-back and the pay multiplier climbs; fail one, or die, and the streak resets. A pristine delivery pays a bonus — a battered one gets docked.

09Two-player deathmatch

From the title screen, press B for 2P Deathmatch — a head-to-head shoot-out in a shared city against a friend.

Getting connected

Link two ThumbyOne handhelds — over the USB cable, on the same LAN, or through an internet room, set up right on the device. Both players spawn into the same generated city with cars to jack and guns to grab.

Winning

Hunt each other down. First to 10 frags takes the match. Cars, weapons and the whole street layout are in play — run your rival over, out-shoot them, or drop a rocket on their ride.

10Field tips

  • Always be selling. The docks turn every finished job's car into cash. Fancy motors and the tank are worth a detour.
  • $100 beats dying. A pay-n-spray clears any heat and fixes your car for less than the cost of losing your whole arsenal to a bust.
  • Pick fights nobody sees. Heat only rises with a witness — no cop in sight means no stars. Check who's watching before you draw.
  • Keep moving under heat. Standing still on foot with stars up gets you arrested fast. In a car you can outrun almost anything below the army.
  • Explore for caches. The best free weapons — flamethrower, rockets — hide in crates out in the districts, and the tank is off the main roads entirely.
  • Read the timer. Job clocks are set from the driving route, so a straight run makes them; wrong turns and crashes don't.
  • Mind your grip. You can't steer at a crawl and heavy cars wash wide — brake before the corner, not in it.
  • The map is your friend. Hit MENU often — it's the only way to see where the phones, shops and docks you've found actually are.
