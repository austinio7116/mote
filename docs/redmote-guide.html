What is RedMote?

RedMote is a complete real-time strategy game on a 128×128 screen. Units are only about six pixels tall, so entire armies fight on-screen at once — but under the hood it's the full RTS: a base to build, an economy to run, a tech tree to climb, fog of war to scout, and an enemy that builds its own base and sends attack waves at you.

You play the blue army; the enemy is red. Win by destroying everything the enemy has — every building and every unit. Lose if the same happens to you.

Controls

Eight buttons do everything. Orders are context-sensitive — the cursor shows a label (MOVE, ATTACK, MINE, RALLY) for what A will do where you're pointing.

D-padMove the cursor. Push it against a screen edge to scroll the map. ATap a unit to select it · tap the ground with a selection to issue the order the cursor shows · drag to box-select a group. A ADouble-tap a unit — select every unit of that type on screen. A A ATriple-tap — select your whole army, map-wide. BDeselect / cancel placement / close a menu. LBOpen the build menu (pauses the game). LB again cycles tabs; D-pad picks a card; A queues or places it; B closes. RBHold for the radar minimap — press A over the map to order or recenter there. A quick tap jumps the view to your base. MENUPause (Resume / Restart / To Title). Hold MENU 3s for the engine's system menu.
Minimap orders: with a group selected, hold RB and press A on the radar map to send them straight to that spot anywhere on the map — you don't have to scroll there first.

Your first five minutes

Nearly every mission (and skirmish) opens the same way. Get the economy running before you build anything else.

  1. Power first. Open the build menu (LB) and put down a Power Plant — most buildings drain power and stall without it.
  2. Refinery next. A Refinery arrives with a free Harvester that immediately drives to the nearest ore and starts banking credits automatically.
  3. Add production. A Barracks for infantry, then a War Factory for tanks (and more harvesters — two per refinery keeps the money flowing).
  4. Tech up. A Radar Dome lights the minimap and unlocks heavier vehicles; a Tech Center opens the Tesla Tank and Tesla Coil.
  5. Defend, then attack. Screen your base with Pillboxes and Gun Turrets, build an army, select it (triple-tap), and push into the enemy.

Economy & power

Ore & crystal

Ore is worth 50 credits per patch; crystal is worth 100 — double value, so contest it. Harvesters fill up to 700 credits, drive back to a Refinery to unload, and repeat forever, all on their own. Mined ground is spent — send harvesters to fresh seams as fields run dry.

Power

Every Power Plant supplies +100. Most other buildings draw power (shown on each build card). If your usage exceeds supply, production slows to a crawl, the radar map goes dark, and Tesla Coils stop firing. The top bar's power meter turns red when you're short — build another Power Plant.

Rule of thumb: keep a little power headroom and two harvesters per refinery. A stalled economy loses games faster than any battle.

What this means

  • Machine-guns & Flamers shred infantry but barely dent tanks — screen them out with your own troops or a Pillbox.
  • Cannons (Light/Heavy Tank, Gun Turret) are the anti-vehicle backbone but can't touch aircraft.
  • Rockets (Rocketeer, Gunship) are the answer to heavy armour and the only reliable anti-air.
  • Artillery out-ranges everything and hits buildings hardest (120%) — but has a 20px minimum range, so keep it behind the line.
  • Tesla deals full damage to everything, armour be damned — expensive, but the ultimate answer.

Commanding your army

Selecting

Tap a unit to pick one, drag a box to grab a group, or triple-tap to select your entire army at once. Double-tap grabs every unit of that type on screen — handy for pulling all your tanks together.

Formations

Order a group to move and they spread into a formation around the target instead of piling onto one tile — they arrive as a battle line, not a scrum.

Attacking

With a selection, point at an enemy and the cursor reads ATTACK. Units chase and fire; artillery and tanks keep their range, infantry close in.

Rally points

Select a Barracks, Factory or Helipad and tap the ground to set a rally point — new units drive there automatically as they roll off the line.

Skirmish mode

A one-off battle against the AI with the setup you choose — both sides get the same start, so it's a fair fight. Tune it from the skirmish screen:

Army

None / Squad / Force / Horde — how big a starting army each side fields, from nothing up to a 22-unit horde for an instant war.

Base

Conyard / Basic / Full — start from a single Construction Yard and build out, or drop into a pre-built base with radar, defences and tech ready.

Funds

$3,000 / $8,000 / $20,000 — starting credits for both sides.

Enemy

Easy / Normal / Hard — the AI builds faster, richer and attacks sooner as you raise it. On Easy it lives on harvesters like you; on Hard it gets a small income bonus.

Field tips

  • Never stop building power. A low-power base can't produce, can't see, and can't fire its Tesla Coils.
  • Two harvesters per refinery. One is never enough once you're building an army.
  • Protect harvesters. They're unarmed and expensive ($800). A couple of raiders can starve your whole economy — a Pillbox near the ore field pays for itself.
  • Mix your army. Rockets for their tanks and air, cannons for their vehicles, flamers/rifles to clear their infantry. A pure tank ball dies to massed rocketeers.
  • Artillery wins sieges but folds in a brawl — always keep it behind heavies.
  • Scout with cheap units. Fog hides everything; a lone rifleman reveals where the enemy is weak before you commit.
  • Contest crystal. At double the value of ore, holding the crystal seam can out-fund the enemy on its own.
  • Queue ahead. Line up several units in the build menu so production never idles while you fight.
