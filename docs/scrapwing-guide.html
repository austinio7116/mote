The Run

A gravity cave-flyer crossed with an R-Type roguelike. Fly a salvaged fighter through procedurally generated alien sectors, strip weapons from the ships you destroy, and splice them into something monstrous. One life. Death is the end of the run.

Thrust, gravity, and hostile caves
Every biome fights differently
Guardians seal the warp every third sector

Sector loop

  • Each sector is a winding cave corridor, generated fresh from your run seed. Fight (or sneak) your way east to the warp tear — a crackling rip in space.
  • The tear's rim colour and the world glowing through it preview the next biome: violet = cavern, red = hive, blue = glacier, green = spore pit, orange = ember forge. You always know what you're warping into.
  • Every third sector is a guardian sector: the tear stays sealed until you destroy the dreadnought that owns it.
  • Difficulty climbs steeply — enemy and boss hull scales superlinearly with sector number, so deep runs demand a fused arsenal, not just reflexes.

Saving

Entering a warp writes a bookmark: quit any time and A RESUME from the title picks up at the start of that sector with your full inventory. It is a bookmark, not a checkpoint — death deletes it. This is a roguelike.

Controls

Your ship is always falling. Flying is throttle management — feather the thrust, don't fight the cave.

D-PADThrust in that direction. LEFT/RIGHT also sets your facing (and firing direction). RBThrust up — a second UP so your thumb never leaves the fire button. AFire the equipped weapon (hold for continuous fire). BCycle to the next weapon in your hold, mid-flight. LBHold to raise the shield — it eats hits while energy lasts and recharges when lowered. MENUOpen the Hangar (equip, inspect, fuse). In a 2P duel: press twice to forfeit.

On the title screen

AStart a run — or resume your bookmarked sector. BAbandon the bookmark and start fresh. < >Choose your airframe from the ships you've unlocked. RB2P DUEL — link up against another pilot. MENUBrowse the Catalogue.
Watch your speed. Slamming into rock above cruising speed costs hull. Gravity is patient; you should be too.

The Fusion Bench

The heart of the game. Two genes go in, one comes out — and the result is live-fired in front of you before you commit.

  • Open the Hangar (MENU), highlight a weapon, press RB to take it to the bench as the base. Pick a core with the D-pad.
  • The result inherits traits from both parents — pattern, element, level growth and mods can all carry across, and fusions can awaken mods neither parent had (marked NEW! in gold).
  • The DMG and RATE bars compare the result against the base: grey is what you already had, the bright segment is the gain, dark red is a loss — with green + / red − numbers.
  • A cycling line spells out what each mod on the result actually does.
  • LB swaps base and core instantly — fusion is order-dependent, so always check both directions.
  • A commits: both parents are consumed, the child is equipped.
Hold economics. Your hold carries 8 weapons. When it's full, new salvage won't fit — fuse aggressively to make room, and to keep your damage curve ahead of the sector scaling.

The Hangar & Run Log

Your pause screen, armoury and record-keeper. The game world holds its breath while you're in here (except in duels — see below).

  • Weapon list: every gene you carry, with icon, name and level. A equips, RB sends it to the Fusion Bench.
  • Stats pane: DMG / RATE bars with exact values, plus projectile speed and mods for the highlighted weapon — the same three lines the bench shows.
  • Run Log (LEFT/RIGHT to flip the pane): kill count and every powerup collected this run as an icon grid. Press DOWN past the last weapon to enter the grid, then A on any powerup for a detail popup explaining exactly what it does.

Biomes & Hazards

Five alien terrains, each with its own enemies, scenery — grown fresh every sector — and two environmental hazards. Hazards telegraph with a shimmer before they strike, appear from sector 2, and multiply as you go deeper.

BiomeTerrainHazards

Hazards don't block the path. Every corridor stays flyable — hazards punish impatience, not existence. Watch the shimmer, time your pass.

Bosses & the Bestiary

417 enemy airframes and 78 dreadnoughts, every one a fixed identity: the same sprite always carries the same class, weapon gene, name and biome.

  • Enemy classes — drifters, hunters, snipers, orbiters, turrets and heavies — are seeded from the sprite itself. Learn a face, know its fight.
  • Mini-bosses (heavies) prowl mid-sector chambers with serious hull.
  • Guardians own every third sector: huge dreadnoughts with a randomized three-move attack deck (fans, rings, spirals, mortar rain, walls, seekers, orbs) that enrages below 35% hull. The warp tear stays sealed until they die — and they drop a levelled weapon, a repair kit and a shield cell.
  • Boss and enemy hull scales superlinearly by sector. Your fused arsenal is the only thing that keeps pace.

The Catalogue & Airframes

A discovery index of everything you've fought: ships, bosses and weapon combos. New finds pop a toast in the corner mid-run.

  • Open from the title (MENU) or the Hangar (LB). LB/RB switch tabs, A opens an info page.
  • Info pages are live: ships and bosses fire their real attack patterns at you in a demo window; bosses lurk in from the right at full resolution.
  • Airframe salvage: destroy 5 of the same enemy airframe and it becomes flyable — a green dot marks it in the catalogue. Select your ship on the title screen with < >. Each airframe brings its own starting weapon gene into the run.

2P Duel

1v1 deathmatch between two linked units — USB cable, LAN or Internet (via Mote Studio). Your collection airframe is your duel ship.

The rules

  • Press RB on the title to open the multiplayer lobby. Once paired, both pilots drop into the same seeded sector — full scenery and hazards, no AI — spawned at the ¼ and ¾ points, facing each other.
  • Weapons, powerups and repair kits are scattered around the arena, with more drifting in as the round runs long. First to find the good salvage wins fights.
  • Any death ends the round — the opponent's guns, a hazard, or the cave itself all score the same. The killer takes the point.
  • Between rounds, the Fusion Bench opens for both pilots. Fuse and equip what you salvaged, then exit — the next round starts the moment both players are ready.
  • Every round is a brand-new sector in a random biome. Inventories carry across rounds; hull and shield reset with 2 seconds of spawn protection.
  • The score runs top-centre. Play to any target you agree on. MENU twice forfeits.
Duel meta. Rounds are short but arsenals persist — sacrificing a round to hoover up salvage and fuse a monster between rounds is a legitimate strategy. So is denying your rival that same pickup.

Pilot's Tips

Feather, don't floor it

Short thrust pulses beat held thrust everywhere except open shafts. Most early deaths are wall impacts, not enemies.

Shield discipline

The shield eats hits but drains fast and only recharges while lowered. Flash it for bullet walls and hazard passes — don't cruise with it up.

Fuse toward one killer

Sector scaling outpaces raw pickups. Funnel your salvage into one high-level gene with mods rather than hoarding eight mediocre ones.

Read the tear

The warp tear shows the next biome. Heading into the ember forge? Spend your repair kit first. Glacier next? Expect vertical icicle lanes.

Salvage with intent

Hunting a specific airframe? Its class and biome are fixed — farm its home biome and check part progress in the catalogue (5 parts = flyable).

Bookmark before bed

The save is written when you warp. If a sector is going badly, surviving to the tear is worth more than any pickup behind you.
