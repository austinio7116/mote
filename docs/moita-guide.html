Getting started

You are a very small wizard at the top of a very deep cave. Somewhere below is a portal. Between you and it: rock, water, oil, acid, molten lava — and things with glowing eyes.

Lava and water pour behind the living title
Depth 3: lava-lit caverns beyond your sight radius
Sparks fly — every projectile lights the cave in its own colour

Each depth is a fresh, procedurally carved cave system with several winding routes down. Reach the pulsing purple portal at the bottom to visit the shop and descend. Deeper levels change biome — more oil, then acid, then open lava — and spawn nastier creatures. Death sends you back to depth 1, so spend your gold while you can.

The dashboard

Everything you need lives in the strip across the top — the world scrolls beneath it, never behind it.

  • ♥ Health bar — you have 100 HP. Enemy contact, enemy bolts, fire and lava all hurt.
  • ⚡ Flight mana — the blue bar under it. Empties as you fly, refills when you land.
  • Coin counter — gold collected this run (see the shop).
  • Wand icons — one tiny procedural icon per wand you carry; the gold frame marks the active one. Every icon is generated from the wand's actual deck, so no two look alike.
  • Wand mana bar — the active wand's charge, in the wand's own colour. Each cast costs mana; wands recharge on their own.
  • D-number — your current depth.

Fog of war

The caves start dark. Your sight radius — plus any glowing thing: lava, fire, a burning wisp, your own projectiles, wands lying in the world — reveals the map permanently. Enemies lurking in unexplored darkness show only as pulsing red eyes. Wands hidden in fog stay hidden until you light the way.

Light is a tool. A glowing spell fired down a dark shaft is the cheapest scouting you'll ever do.

Wands & casting

Wands are decks of spell cards, cast in order. The whole system is built for combining.

Every wand has a deck (up to 8 cards), a cast delay, and a mana pool with its own recharge rate. When you cast, the wand reads its deck left to right from a moving cursor: modifier cards are consumed and accumulated until the first projectile card — which fires with all of the accumulated modifiers applied. The cursor then moves on, wrapping at the end.

  • D+ 3X FI — every cast fires a triple fan of boosted fireballs.
  • SP D+ SP — alternates a plain spark and a damage-boosted spark.
  • TW EC 5X MT — a twin, echoing, five-way meteor storm. Mind the mana bill.

Mana cost scales with what you stack — damage, extra shots, explosions and pierce all add up. A greedy combo can outrun its wand's recharge.

Wands found lying in the caves — marked by a slowly wheeling starburst in the wand's colour — are free. Walk into one to take it (you carry up to three).

The wand editor

Press MENU at any time — the world freezes while you work.

  • ▲ ▼ choose a wand, ◀ ▶ choose a slot.
  • A picks up the card under the cursor, drops a carried card, or swaps the two — move cards freely between wands to build combos.
  • MENU / B closes the editor. Decks compact automatically and a card in hand is always re-homed — nothing is ever lost.
Order matters. Modifiers only affect the projectile that comes after them in the deck. Front-load your modifiers.

Gold & the shop

Enemies burst into coins. Coins magnet to you. Gold does nothing — until the portal.

Touching the portal opens the between-levels shop: three fresh wands (shown as their icons) and three loose spell cards, each priced in gold. Prices show gold when you can afford them, red when you can't.

◀ ▶ ▲ ▼Browse the six slots ABuy — cards slot into your current wand's free space BDone — descend to the next depth
Spend it. Gold doesn't survive death. A run that dies rich died twice.

Field notes

  • Water beats lava. One Water card turns a molten lake into a walkable obsidian bridge.
  • Oil is a weapon. Splat an Oil Blob under a chokepoint, retreat, and flick a Spark at it.
  • Dig is mobility. A Dig wand tunnels routes the cave never offered — and drops enemies into hazards below.
  • Vine up, ice across. Vine Seed grows climbable wood; Ice Shard freezes a pond into a floor.
  • The Void Orb eats walls. Fired at the ground it digs a pit; fired at a crowd it collects them first.
  • Leech + Swarm is a health plan. Mana Refund + Spark is an economy plan.
  • Flight mana only refills on the ground. Don't hover over acid with an empty bar.
