Getting started

You are a very small wizard at the top of a very deep cave. Somewhere below is a portal. Between you and it: rock, water, oil, acid, molten lava — and things with glowing eyes. Everything is simulated pixel by pixel, and your wands don't just fire spells — they brew them.

Lava and water pour behind the living title
Fog-shrouded caverns lit only by your magic and the lava
Every projectile lights the cave in its own colour

Reach the pulsing purple portal at the bottom of each depth to visit the shop and descend. Deeper levels change biome and spawn nastier creatures. Death sends you back to depth 1 — so spend your gold while you have it.

The dashboard

Everything lives in the strip across the top — the world scrolls beneath it.

  • ♥ Health — 100 HP. Enemy contact, enemy shots, fire, lava and acid all hurt.
  • ⚡ Flight mana — the blue bar; empties as you fly, refills when you land.
  • Coins — gold this run, spent at the shop.
  • Wand icons — one procedural icon per wand; the gold frame marks the active one.
  • Wand mana — the active wand's charge, in its own colour. Each cast costs mana.

How casting works

A wand is not a list of separate shots — it is a recipe. Pull the trigger and the whole deck compiles into one compound spell.

Three rules govern the brew, and all three turn on the order of your cards:

  1. Every modifier applies to the whole cast. A single Damage+ anywhere boosts everything you fire.
  2. Adjacent projectile cards fuse. Two projectiles next to each other merge into one carrier that blends their elements (see Fusion). A modifier placed between them breaks the fusion, so they fire as separate shots instead.
  3. A trigger nests the rest as a payload. The first trigger card splits the deck — everything after it becomes a payload that fires later (see Triggers).
Order is everything. Fire Water Bolt fuses all three into a steam-brewing bolt. Fire Bolt Water fires a burning bolt and a separate water shot. Same three cards — completely different spell.

Fusion — the DNA of a shot

When projectile cards sit side by side, their elemental "genes" merge into one carrier. Certain gene pairs brew emergent effects on impact — this is where the magic lives.

Gene pairs

CombineBrews
Fire + WaterSteam burst (cloud, knockback, douses fire)
Lava + WaterObsidian shrapnel (hard fragments + damage)
Fire + IceThermal shatter (big bonus damage on the hit)

Any projectiles fuse — the carrier's motion comes from the first card in the run, the effects from all of them. So a Fire+Ice Needle is a fast railgun that thermal-shatters, while Fire+Ice Bomb lobs and shatters on landing.

Triggers — spells inside spells

A trigger card splits your deck. Everything after it stops being part of the main shot and becomes a payload — its own little fused volley — fired when the carrier reaches its moment.

OperatorFires the payload…
▸ On Impactwhere the carrier hits terrain or an enemy
▸ On Timermid-air, a fraction of a second after launch (airbursts)
▸ On Deathwhen the carrier expires or is destroyed (radial burst)
Payloads fuse too. The cards after a trigger follow the same fusion rules, so Bolt ▸Impact Fire Water throws a bolt that releases a steam burst where it lands.

Fog of war & barrels

Caves start dark. Your sight and any glowing thing reveal the map permanently; enemies in unexplored dark show only as glowing eyes. Scattered oil kegs and powder kegs detonate when shot, burned, or caught in a blast — and chain-react into each other.

Gold & the shop

Enemies burst into coins that magnet to you. Gold is spent at the portal.

Touching the portal opens the Wand Shop: three wands and three loose spell cards, each priced. Buy wands to expand your loadout and cards to feed your recipes, then descend. Wands found lying in the caves (inside a wheeling starburst) are free.

Spend it. Gold doesn't survive death.

Field notes

  • Think in pairs. Two elements next to each other almost always brew something better than either alone.
  • A modifier is also a divider. Slip one between two projectiles to keep them as separate shots instead of fusing.
  • Water is a weapon and a shield. Fire+Water clouds blind and knock back; Spark+Water over a pool wipes a room.
  • Lay traps. Oil Blob or Napalm on a chokepoint, then a Fire shot — or an On-Timer airburst overhead.
  • Triggers multiply everything. A cheap carrier with a fat payload out-damages a fat carrier.
  • Flight mana only refills on the ground — don't hover over lava with an empty bar.
