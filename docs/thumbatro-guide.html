THUMBATRO

Play poker hands to beat the score. Warp your deck. Take one more round.
by austinio7116 · single player · poker roguelike

The Goal

Each round sets a score to beat. You have 4 hands and 4 discards to reach it by playing poker hands from a standard 52-card deck. Clear the target and you open a booster pack to warp your deck, then the next round demands more. Miss it and the run is over — your best single hand is your trophy.

  • Round 1 target is 300. Every round after adds +500 (800, 1300, 1800…).
  • Hands and discards reset to 4 each new round, and the deck reshuffles.
  • Run out of hands below target — or run the deck dry — and it's Game Over (press A to start again).

Playing a Hand

You hold 8 cards. Move the pointer with LEFT/RIGHT, UP to select a card and DOWN to deselect — up to five. Then:

  • A — Play the selected cards as a poker hand and bank the score.
  • B — Discard the selected cards and draw replacements (costs one discard).

Either way you redraw to refill the gap, so the deck thins as the round runs. The HUD shows score / target, the current hand's base and mult, the hand type, and your remaining hands, discards and round.

Discards are for fishing — dump three dead cards to chase the flush. But every discard is one you can't get back, and the deck only holds what's left.

Scoring

A hand scores base × mult. The poker rank sets the starting base and mult; then every scoring card adds its rank value (Ace = 14, face = 11–13) plus any card modifiers, and your jokers pile on. Bigger ranks pay more base:

A fresh deck is just these — 52 ordinary cards. Everything below is what the packs add on top.

Warping the Deck

Clear a round and the booster pack deals 4–7 cards face up. Take one or two (UP/DOWN to pick, A to confirm). What you can find:

Special cards (go into your deck)

Bonus, Mult, Wild and Coin cards keep their rank and suit — they're ordinary cards with a perk, so they still form pairs and flushes. A Steel card wants to sit in your hand unplayed; a Coin card wants to be played, to fatten the next pack.

Hand upgrades (planet-style)

Permanently level a specific poker hand: each level adds +10 base and +1 mult to that hand every time you make it. Level your bread-and-butter hand and it snowballs.

Tarots (reshape the deck)

Thinning the deck is a strategy: fewer, better cards means you draw your scoring hand more often. Cut a suit you never flush, and every draw improves.

Jokers

Jokers are passive scoring engines that fire on every hand. You can hold two, and that's a hard cap: once both slots are full, jokers in later packs are locked — you can't take one, and there's no way to drop or swap a joker you already hold. Pick your pair carefully, they're with you for the run. View them any time with LB/RB.

Conditional jokers — pay per matching card you play

Each pays out once for every selected card that matches its rule. Common conditional jokers add chips per match; rarer ones add mult per match.

Flat jokers — a fixed bonus, once per hand

Build a joker around the deck you're shaping: an Even-mult joker loves a deck you've upranked toward even ranks; a Face joker pays off when you keep the face cards — Jacks, Queens and Kings — in your deck instead of cutting them with tarots.
