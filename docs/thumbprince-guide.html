THUMBPRINCE

Draft the estate. Reach the Antechamber. Leave your mark.
by austinio7116 · single player · roguelike drafting

The Day

The estate is a blank 5×8 blueprint. You start each day in the Entrance Hall at the bottom with 20 steps — every doorway you pass through costs one. The Antechamber waits at the top behind a triple-locked golden door (three keys at once, or the Master Key). Enter it and the day is won; run out of steps and the day ends where you stand. Either way, tomorrow the blueprint is blank again — only your best score survives.

Drafting

Walk into a door that leads to an unexplored cell and the drafting desk opens: the full blueprint on the left, a hand of room cards on the right. The selected room is previewed live in its cell — doors and all — before you commit. A places it and walks you in.

  • Corridors (Hallway, the Walks, Crossroads…) are cheap and open the estate — real walled passages, not empty squares.
  • Dead-end rooms carry the loot: steps, keys, gems, stars.
  • Green rooms pay gems, and score +25 for each green neighbour.
  • Fancy rooms cost gems; the Vault costs a key to even draft.
  • Every offer is guaranteed at least one free pick.
A drafted room's doors are placed relative to where you came in — a dead end against the estate edge wastes nothing but the steps it took to reach it. Watch the preview.

Doors, Keys & Keycards

Locks get likelier the higher you climb. A plain lock takes one key — found on floors, in chests, or sold by the Key Smith and Tuck Shop. Near the top, some locks are keycard readers instead, and keys mean nothing to them:

  • The rare Keycard (locked chests, or 18 gold at the Key Smith) reads green forever.
  • A Security room terminal arms a one-door override.
  • The Power Room breaker kills every reader for the day — but the halls dim and ordinary locks stiffen.

Treasure chests open with a bump; padlocked ones take a key and pay better — gold, gems, keys, stars, sometimes the Keycard itself.

The Estate Lives

  • Dig spots — a twinkle on the floor; stand on it and press A (Spade required) for gold, gems, keys, stars or a snack.
  • Riddle notes — crumpled paper pointing at a glinting cache near a drafted room ("N OF KITCHEN", pinned to the map). Go there: +75 and gold.
  • Day conditions — dealt at dawn: Frosty greens, Creaky locks, Generous chests, Market prices, Bright ranks, Restful rooms, Miser coins, Lucky digs.
  • Cursed rooms — the Crypt drains four steps at the door; the Trick Hall seals shut behind you.

Scoring

  • Rooms score by rarity; coins, stars, sweeps and chests add up.
  • Adjacency combos: Kitchen beside Dining is a Service Wing, bedroom beside Washroom an En-Suite, Library beside Study the Archive — and placing a room's twin mirrored across the centre column is Matched Wings.
  • The chain: every placement that scores any bonus raises a ×2–×4 multiplier on placement bonuses; a dry placement breaks it.
  • Ranks: complete a full row of five for +100.
  • Two daily goals (on the map screen) pay 100–150 each.
  • Winning pays +500 and converts leftover steps, keys, gems and gold. The results screen itemises where every point came from.
